Economic Development and Air Quality (2020-2023)

Key Insights

  • Strong negative correlation (r = -0.68) between GDP per capita and PM2.5 levels: wealthier nations have cleaner air
  • Countries with GDP above $40,000 per capita consistently maintain PM2.5 below 15 µg/m³ through strict regulations
  • Middle-income countries ($10,000-$30,000 GDP) show wide variation, suggesting policy effectiveness matters more than wealth alone
  • Low-income nations face a dual challenge: limited resources for clean technology and rapid industrial growth
