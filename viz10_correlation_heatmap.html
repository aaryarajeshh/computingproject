Key Insights

  • Strongest correlations: PM2.5 with respiratory deaths (0.72), PM2.5 with COPD (0.68), and GDP with life expectancy (0.82)
  • GDP per capita shows strong negative correlation with PM2.5 (-0.68), confirming that economic development enables cleaner air
  • Urban population has weak positive correlation with PM2.5 (0.45), showing urbanization alone doesn't determine air quality
  • Health outcomes (life expectancy, respiratory deaths) are more strongly linked to air quality than to GDP alone, highlighting pollution as an independent health determinant
