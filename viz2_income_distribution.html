PM2.5 Distribution by Income Level

Environmental Inequality Analysis

Previous: Global Trends Home Next: Top Countries

Key Insights

  • Low-income countries experience median PM2.5 levels of 65 µg/m³, nearly 5 times worse than high-income countries (14 µg/m³)
  • Middle-income countries show the highest variability, with some approaching WHO guidelines while others exceed 80 µg/m³
  • High-income countries have successfully implemented air quality regulations, with 75% staying below 20 µg/m³
  • The air quality gap between rich and poor nations is widening, representing a critical environmental justice issue
