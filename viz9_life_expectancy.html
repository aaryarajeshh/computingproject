Key Insights

  • Strong negative correlation (r = -0.65) between PM2.5 and life expectancy: cleaner air means longer lives
  • Countries with PM2.5 below 10 µg/m³ have average life expectancy of 81 years, compared to 68 years in heavily polluted nations
  • Reducing PM2.5 by 10 µg/m³ could add approximately 0.6 years to life expectancy on average
  • Air pollution accounts for an estimated 13-year gap in life expectancy between cleanest and most polluted countries
