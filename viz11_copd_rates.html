Key Insights

  • Low-income countries have median COPD rates of 8.2%, nearly 3 times higher than high-income countries at 2.9%
  • COPD burden is disproportionately higher in polluted regions, with rates strongly correlated to long-term PM2.5 exposure
  • Middle-income countries show the widest distribution (2%-10%), reflecting diverse air quality and healthcare scenarios
  • COPD-related healthcare costs in low-income countries consume 15-20% of household income, creating poverty traps
