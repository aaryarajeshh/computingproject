Decade Comparison: 2010s vs 2020s

Air Quality and Health by Income Level

Previous: COPD Rates Home

Key Insights

  • Low-income countries saw PM2.5 increase by 8% from 2010s to 2020s (59.8 to 64.7 µg/m³), while high-income nations improved by 15%
  • Respiratory deaths rose 12% in low-income countries but fell 18% in high-income countries over the same period
  • The air quality gap between rich and poor nations widened significantly over the past decade, exacerbating global health inequality
  • Without intervention, projections suggest low-income countries will face 30% higher PM2.5 levels by 2030 compared to 2020
