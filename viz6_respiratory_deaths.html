PM2.5 vs Respiratory Deaths

Health Impact Analysis (2020-2023)

Previous: Urbanization Home Next: Asthma Rates

Key Insights

  • Strong positive correlation (r = 0.72) between PM2.5 exposure and respiratory deaths: each 10 µg/m³ increase in PM2.5 corresponds to approximately 8 additional deaths per 100,000 people
  • Countries with PM2.5 above 60 µg/m³ experience respiratory death rates exceeding 80 per 100,000, nearly 5x higher than clean-air nations
  • Low-income countries face compounded risk: high pollution plus limited healthcare access leads to preventable deaths
  • WHO estimates 7 million premature deaths annually worldwide from air pollution exposure
