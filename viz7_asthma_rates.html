Key Insights

  • Children in hazardous air quality zones have asthma rates averaging 12.5%, compared to 4.2% in good air quality areas
  • Clear dose-response relationship: as air quality worsens from good to hazardous, asthma prevalence nearly triples
  • Poor air quality regions show wider variability in asthma rates, suggesting some populations are more vulnerable than others
  • Long-term PM2.5 exposure during childhood increases lifetime asthma risk by 40-60%
